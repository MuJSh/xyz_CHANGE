On December 27, Shandong University (Qingdao) announced the approved 2025–2026 student innovation and entrepreneurship projects. The project submitted by the Institute of Environment Research, "Satellite remote-sensing multi-source data deep fusion and rice-yield prediction for digital agricultural engineering," was successfully approved as a key funded project.

![Approved 2025-2026 student innovation and entrepreneurship project list]({{ '/assets/img/news/chengwen-funding.jpg' | relative_url }})

*Approved 2025–2026 student innovation and entrepreneurship project list.*

The project focuses on national strategies for rural revitalization, Digital China, and key technology needs in digital and smart agriculture. It will use multi-source satellite remote-sensing data as the foundation and introduce artificial-intelligence technologies such as deep learning to conduct data-fusion and prediction analyses. The team aims to build a high-accuracy rice-yield prediction model, explore intelligent perception and quantitative characterization methods for crop growth under complex agricultural environments, and provide technical support for smart agricultural production and precision management.

The project is led by Chengwen Niu, a master's student in the research group. Undergraduate students Yuxuan An and Mengyue Ma are project members. Two additional members come from the School of Information Science and Engineering and the School of Integrated Circuits.

According to the announcement, 70 project applications were submitted in this round, and 33 were approved. Among them, 3 projects received key funding, 10 received general funding, and 20 received cultivation funding. The successful approval of this project as a key funded project, ranking first in the list, reflects the group's active exploration in remote sensing and big data for environmental research. The project will further promote multidisciplinary integration, deepen the intersection of remote sensing, artificial intelligence, and agricultural-environment research, serve agricultural modernization, and build on the group's existing research foundation.
