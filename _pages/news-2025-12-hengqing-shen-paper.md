In a new study published by Xuanying Ji and collaborators, the PVLIB-Python model was used to quantify changes in photovoltaic (PV) capacity factors and power generation across China due to the Clean Air Action Plan from 2013 to 2020.

The study finds that improved air quality has substantially enhanced PV generation, particularly in eastern China. Based on current PV capacity, this improvement can generate up to 41.3 TWh of additional electricity per year. The largest increases occur in provinces such as Shandong and Henan, where air pollution levels and PV installation capacity are both relatively high.

By using multiple CMIP6 models, the study also estimates potential future changes in PV power generation in China. Under sustainable-development pathways and strong climate-change mitigation policies, reduced air pollution and increased solar radiation could increase PV power generation by 37.4–52.6 TWh per year by the 2050s, even without considering newly installed PV facilities.

This work is a collaboration between Shandong University and Duke University.
